# Corporate Organizational Structure

A general corporate organizational structure from ownership level to operational workforce.

---

# 0. Ownership & Governance

## 0.1 Founder
- Establishes the company
- Defines vision and mission
- Initiates business strategy
- May also serve as executive management

## 0.2 Shareholders
- Own company shares/equity
- Approve major corporate decisions
- Appoint Board members
- Receive dividends and reports

## 0.3 Board of Commissioners / Board of Supervisors
- Oversees executive management
- Monitors corporate governance
- Advises the Board of Directors
- Represents shareholder interests

## 0.4 Independent Commissioner
- Provides independent oversight
- Ensures transparency and compliance
- Prevents conflicts of interest

## 0.5 Audit Committee
- Reviews financial reporting
- Monitors internal controls
- Oversees audit processes
- Supports corporate compliance

---

# 1. C-Level / Executive Structure

## 1.1 Executive Committee / Board of Directors

### 1.1.1 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Leads overall company operations
- Defines strategic direction
- Oversees all executive divisions
- Reports to shareholders and commissioners

### 1.1.2 Chief Operating Officer (COO)
- Manages daily operations
- Ensures operational efficiency
- Coordinates operational departments

### 1.1.3 Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Oversees financial strategy
- Manages budgeting and investments
- Controls financial reporting

### 1.1.4 Chief Technology Officer (CTO)
- Leads technology development
- Oversees engineering and innovation
- Defines technical direction

### 1.1.5 Chief Information Officer (CIO)
- Manages IT infrastructure
- Oversees internal information systems
- Ensures cybersecurity and data management

### 1.1.6 Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)
- Leads marketing strategy
- Manages branding and communication
- Oversees market campaigns

### 1.1.7 Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)
- Oversees human resources
- Develops workforce strategy
- Manages employee relations

### 1.1.8 Chief Strategy Officer (CSO)
- Leads long-term strategic planning
- Analyzes business opportunities
- Supports organizational growth

### 1.1.9 Chief Product Officer (CPO)
- Oversees product development
- Defines product strategy
- Aligns products with business goals

### 1.1.10 Chief Risk Officer (CRO)
- Identifies business risks
- Develops risk mitigation strategies
- Ensures regulatory compliance

### 1.1.11 Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)
- Ensures legal and policy compliance
- Oversees regulatory frameworks
- Monitors corporate ethics

### 1.1.12 Chief Data Officer (CDO)
- Manages corporate data strategy
- Oversees analytics and governance
- Supports data-driven decisions

### 1.1.13 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Oversees cybersecurity
- Protects company systems and information
- Manages security policies
